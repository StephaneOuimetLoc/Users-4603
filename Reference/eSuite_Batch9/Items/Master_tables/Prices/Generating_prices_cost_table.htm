eSuite - How to Generate Prices Using the Cost Table

eSuite > Items

Information

The Generate price utility is used to calculate the selling prices from the cost amounts entered in the Cost table. The calculation is based on a margin.

Displaying the Generate price window

The Generate price window can be opened manually or automatically when a change is made in the Cost table. See the reseller for more details.

* Click on a field to get information on it

NOTE: If the Contract price table is opened, the customer prices based on a margin will also be generated.

The Margin field

The Margin field contains the profit margin on the item in percentage. It is used to generate the selling price.
The default value is Item.

The Margin field in the Generate price window has priority over the Margin field in the Price table and overwrites it. However, the Generate price window can use the margin value of the Price table. To do so, the Item value must be entered in the Margin field of the Generate price window. A margin value must be entered in the Price table for a selling price to be calculated.

By default, the profit is calculated from the selling price and not from the cost of the item(s). For example, if the cost of the item is $1.00 and the margin is 10%, the item would be sold at $1.10. However, the generated price is $1.11. The system uses the following formula: for a profit of 10%, the cost is considered as 90% of the selling price. If 90% is equal to $1.00, 100% (the selling price) is equal to $1.11.

See the dealer for more details on how the profit may be calculated from the cost instead of the selling price and how the profit on the suggested retail price may be calculated from the retail price instead of the cost.

To enter the profit margin:

  • Type the profit margin in percentage in the Margin field, or
  • Type Item in the Margin field to use the profit margin entered in the TGM field of the Price table.
    The generated selling price is automatically displayed in the Price field of the Generate price window.

The Round field

The Round field contains the ending number of the selling price. It works in combination with the Margin field.
Its default value is Item.

Two different ending numbers separated by a comma may be specified in the Round field. In this case:

  • Any selling price that ends with a 5 or less will end with the first ending number.
  • Any selling price that ends above a 5 will end with the second ending number.

The Round field in the Generate price window has priority over the Round field in the Price table and overwrites it. However, the Generate price window can use the rounding value of the Price table. To do so, the Item value must be entered in the Round field of the Generate price window. A rounding value must be entered in the Price table for the ending number to apply.

To enter the ending number of the selling price:

  • Type the ending number(s) in the Round field, or
  • Type Item in the Round field to use the ending number(s) entered in the Round field of the Price table.
    The generated selling price is automatically updated in the Price field of the Generate price window.

Generating a price

The Generate price utility helps generate the selling prices and the future selling prices from the cost amounts, based on a profit margin.

The selling prices may be generated item by item, globally for a group of items or automatically for all items. The Generate price window can be opened manually or automatically as soon as a change is made in the Cost table. See the dealer for more details.

To generate a selling price, follow these steps:

  1. Open the Generate price window.
  2. Change the targets affected, if necessary.
  3. Change the price levels affected, if necessary, or
    Click on the All levels button to specify all price levels defined in the system.
  4. Select the cost from which the selling price is generated (base, landed or net cost).
    The cost value of the selected base is displayed in the Cost field of the Generate price window.
  5. Enter the profit margin.
    The generated selling price is displayed in the Price field.
  6. Enter the rounding value, if necessary.
    The generated selling price is updated in the Price field.
  7. Specify the selling price generated.
  8. Click on the appropriate button to generate the specified selling price.

Generating a price from the TGM field

The Retail, Package and Limited prices may be generated directly from the TGM fields of the Price, Future price, Future sales, Future TPR and Future instore tables.

NOTES:
  • To be able to generate a selling price in the Price table, a cost must exist for the item in the Cost table.
  • To be able to generate a future selling price, a cost must exist for the item in the Cost table or a future cost must exist in the Future cost table.

To generate a retail, package or limited price, follow these steps:

  1. Enter the desired margin value in the corresponding TGM field.
  2. Enter the price quantity in the corresponding Price field.
    A price quantity is required to generate a price.
  3. Enter the ending number in the corresponding Round field, if necessary.
  4. To generate the selling price, click on name of button or double-click in the corresponding TGM field (in the Price table only).
    The Generate price window is displayed.
  5. Select the cost from which the selling price is generated (from which the profit is made).
    The unit cost amount is displayed in the bottom of the Generate price window.
  6. Click on the appropriate button to generate the corresponding selling price.
