Customer table

The eSuite software uses relational tables to store customer information. The Customer table contains general information on the customer, such as name, address, phone numbers, etc.

Displaying the Customer table

In the Launcher, click the arrow in the Customers entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Clients: the Start window opens in the Customer work area.
  2. Click on General List: the Wizard tab opens in the same window, displaying the Customers - General List.
  3. Enter the search criteria and click Execute: results will display in a record grid, in the Customers tab of the same window.
  4. To modify an existing customer record, select it in the list and double-click on it, press the F3 key on the keyboard or click the Edit command in the bottom menu: the Customer table opens, overlapping the work area.
  5. To create a new customer record, click on Add in the bottom menu of the grid: the Customer table opens, overlapping the work area.

The Valid until field

The Valid until field contains the date on which the customer's account becomes inactive.

The Customer not found message is displayed in the Entry window of the POS when the cashier enters an inactive customer account number.

Promotion

The Valid until field also works in combination with the Promo field. In the case where a promotion shopper level is assigned to the customer, the Valid until field sets the promotion validation date. When the promotion period expires on the date defined in the Valid until field, the customer account goes back to its original shopper level.

The Status field

The Status field contains the customer's account status.

 

The Status field is used to filter the customer account number in the Receivable module, except for those that are not active. This feature may be useful when a customer account history is needed on file, but the account is no longer active.

The Status field is also used to do not mail the electronic receipt (R) to this customer when the system is set up with the electronic receipt feature.

The POS comment field

The POS comment field contains a comment, a message or a piece of information that is displayed on the cashier display when the customer number is entered.

The POS comment field can be a convenient way to make the cashier aware of the customer's status or any other pertinent remark concerning the customer.

Note:
Both the points of sale and the customer screens need to be designed depending on the use of the POS comment.
Some of the screens are used for the cashier screen and the customer screen already has the POS comment field by default. If the display on the cashier screen is not supported, operators can still enter comments or messages for the office use.

The Point programs field

The Point programs field in the Customer table is used within the eCommerce solution. It works on an opt-in/opt-out basis, allowing customers to choose the programs they want to participate in, according to their shopper level. When the customer is part of all point programs that correspond to the shopper level they belong to, the Point programs field is empty.

The Point programs field contains the identifier characters of the point programs the customer is member of. The customer account must be linked to a shopper level.

The system can manage up to 35 point programs. They are identified by characters from 1 to 9 and from A to Z: A corresponds to the point program 10, B corresponds to the point program 11 a.s.o., up to Z that corresponds to the point program 35.

The point program 1 is the main point program. All other point programs are called 'secondary point programs'.

Multiple point programs may be specified, case in which the characters in the field should not be separated by any space, comma or semi-colon.  

Records must already exist in the Point programs field of the Shopper level table for the Point programs field to display choices.
