eSuite - Price Table

eSuite > Items > Master > Item list

Information

The eSuite software uses relational tables to store item information. The Price table contains all pricing information for the main item.

To find a price record for the main item, eSuite uses a unique key made up of three fields: the main item UPC, the target and the price level. This means that each UPC can have more than one price record in the Price table, as long as the target and/or the price level change. For the same target, a UPC can have as many different price records as there are unique price levels, and for the same price level, a UPC can have as many different price records as there are unique targets.

Displaying the Price Table

In the Launcher, click the arrow in the Items entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Master: the Start window opens in the Items work area.
  2. Click on Item list: the Wizard tab opens in the same window, displaying the Items - General List view.
  3. Enter the search criteria and click Execute: results will display in a record grid, in the Items tab of the same window.
  4. Select a record.
  5. Click on Edit on the bottom of the item grid to display its menu.
  6. Select Master tables, then Price master: the Price edit table opens over the work area.

Because of the multiple records capability, the Price table can toggle between a simple view and a detailed view.

The simple view consists of the quick reference section and the pricing columns.
The detailed view adds the bottom tab section and the left tab section.

The Collapse/Expand left control in the toolbar hides/shows the left tab section.
The Collapse/Expand bottom control in the toolbar hides/shows the bottom tab section.

Quick item reference

The quick item reference section has two editable fields: Target (with a multi-store installation) and Level. They fields must be filled in before adding prices.

 

The other fields are grayed out and used for information purposes only:

  • The Active price field shows the current price for the item.
  • The Next price field will show the new price until the changes are deployed to the points of sale. Once the modifications have been deployed, the Active price field is updated with the value in the Next price field and the Next price field is updated with the next pricing event, based on the pricing columns.

Pricing columns

The pricing columns allow maintaining several pricing events:

  • Regular: displays the normal (regular) price of the item.
  • TPR: displays the temporary price reduction applied on the item.
  • Sale: displays the short term promotional price of the item.
  • Store: displays store-specific price (temporary or permanent) of the item.

Each pricing column contains several common fields: Price, Start date, End date. The start and end dates are inclusive: regardless of the price column, the prices in the Price fields will remain active until the end date expires.

The Regular and the Sale columns display the Time field.

The TPR column displays the Ad field, containing the identification letter of the advertising type used for the item.

The Store column displays the TPR and Sale checkboxes. The TPR checkbox specifies whether the store price overrides the TPR price (checked) or not (unchecked). The Sale field specifies whether the store price overrides all prices (checked) or not (unchecked).

Precedence

If Regular, TPR and Sale pricing columns contain prices but none of the three has the start and end dates specified, the Regular price takes precedence over the two other ones.

If a Store price is also entered (without any dates specified), the Store price takes precedence over the Regular price. Both TPR and Sale prices require valid dates in the Start and End date fields for their prices to take effect. If no dates are specified for the Regular price, the eSuite software uses the date and time when the modifications were made as the active date for the change. The TPR price takes precedence over the Regular price when the current day is part of the TPR dates range. The Sale price also takes precedence over the Regular price when the current day is part of the Sale dates range. If the current day is part of both the TPR and Sale dates ranges, the Sale price takes precedence over the TPR price. Finally, the Store price may also take precedence over the TPR and Sale prices if it is specified as such.

Default precedence Store Precedence over Sale (includes over TPR) Store Precedence Over TPR

Profit margin calculation method

The profit margin is displayed for each price, near the header for each column.

The profit margin is calculated according to one of the two options that were selected when the software was configured for your store. To illustrate the two options available, here are two examples with the same cost and profit margin %.

Margin on cost

  • The margin on cost in this example is the result of 25% of $10.00.
  • The margin is $2.50, and the retail price is $12.50.
  • The formula to calculate the profit margin as a percentage of the cost is : Cost + (Cost * profit margin %).

Margin on retail

  • The margin on retail in this example is the result of 25% of the retail price.
  • The margin is $3.33 and the retail price is $13.33.
  • The formula to calculate the profit margin as a percentage of the retail price is: Cost / (1 - profit margin %).

Bottom tab section

The bottom tab section offers additional information on the price of the item in the Price table. The following tabs and views are available: Base (displayed by default), Margin, Redeem and General. See the Layout section for details.

Price table grid

The Price table grid offers quick information on the price record. The following tabs and views are available: Grid (displayed by default), Active and Next.

The Grid tab shows the item price records displayed in a list with four columns: Target, Level, Active price and Quantity. Information in the grid is not editable.

Columns are movable, resizable and may be selected/deselected for a customized experience. They allow sorting the records in an ascending or a descending order.
Records are displayed in ascending order by target, with the PAL target record selected by default. Selecting a record allows displaying and editing its information in the quick item reference section of the Price table:

The Active tab contains data on the active price of the record selected in the Grid tab. Information is displayed in a table and is not editable.

The Next tab contains data on the next price of the record selected in the Grid tab. Information is displayed in a table and is not editable.

The Target field

The Target field contains the target identifier, namely the store group. It is a required field, meaning that new price records must have a valid entry in the Target field for the record to exist. It is used as a key field, along with the main item UPC and the Level field to uniquely identify records in the Price grid (left tab section of the table). The default value of the Target field is PAL.

The eSuite software is designed to support multi-stores or regional pricing. The Target field is not editable unless the Multi target option is selected in the System.ini file.

In a multi-stores setting, the target identifier can be modified by typing it in the Target field or by double-clicking the field and selecting the target in the Target grid.

Records must already exist in the Target grid for choices to be available. A warning message is displayed for invalid target identifiers.

Back to layout

The Level field

The Level field contains the price level number. It is a required field, i.e. new price records must have a valid entry in the Level field for the record to exist. It is used as a key field, along with the main item UPC and the Target field to uniquely identify records in the Price grid (left tab section of the table). The default value of the Level field is 1.

NOTE:
Starting with SMS v350x, Application.ini[TARGET_TRS] is used to determine which target to use for each item table.
Application.ini[TARGET_TRS]PRICE_TAB='PAL' will force the Host to use the PAL record.
Application.ini[TARGET_TRS]PRICE_TAB='@WIZGET(TARGET)' will force the Host to use the Global Store Number record.
Application.ini[TARGET_TRS]PRICE_TAB=ISNULL((SELECT TOP 1 SUB_PRI.F1000 FROM PRICE_TAB SUB_PRI JOIN LNK_TAB SUB_LNK ON SUB_PRI.F1000=SUB_LNK.F1000 JOIN LNK_TAB SUB_LNK2 ON SUB_LNK.F1056=SUB_LNK2.F1056 AND SUB_LNK.F1057=SUB_LNK2.F1057 AND SUB_LNK2.F1000='@WIZGET(TARGET)' JOIN STO_TAB SUB_STO ON SUB_LNK.F1000=SUB_STO.F1000 WHERE SUB_PRI.F01=PRI.F01 AND SUB_PRI.F126=PRI.F126 ORDER BY SUB_STO.F1937 DESC),'PAL'). That will use the proper target set for the Global StoreID selected.

The Active price field

The Active price field shows the current active price for the item, that is the current price for the record target and price level.

The Active price field is not editable and used for information purposes only. It contains the price at which the item is selling at the points of sale included in the target unless there were communication errors. If the item has never been deployed, i.e. sent to the points of sale, the Active price field is empty.

The Active price field, as all other price fields, is split into two (2) fields:

  1. The Active price amount field that contains the current active price for the item, and
  2. The Active price quantity field that contains the current active price quantity for the item.

The Next price field

The Next price field shows the next price for the item based on the modifications made in the pricing columns.

The Next price field is not editable and used for information purposes only. Its value is updated as soon as the modifications are posted (saved) to the database. If there are several new prices (TPR or sales), the Next price field shows the next pricing event based on price column priorities. Once the modifications have been deployed to the points of sale, the Active price field is updated with the Next price field information and the Next price field is updated to display the next pricing event based on the modifications made in the pricing columns.

The Next price field, as all other price fields, is split into two (2) fields:

The Price field

The Price table field contains the selling prices for the pricing events.

The Price field is split into two fields for each pricing column:

Regular pricing column

  1. The Regular Price amount contains the retail price for the item.
  2. The Regular Price quantity contains the retail price quantity for the item.

TPR pricing column

  1. The TPR Price amount contains the TPR price for the item.
  2. The TPR Price quantity contains the TPR price quantity for the item.

Sale pricing column

  1. The Sale Price amount contains the sale price for the item.
  2. The Sale Price quantity contains the sale price quantity for the item.

Store pricing column

  1. The Store Price amount contains the instore price for the item.
  2. The Store Price quantity contains the instore price quantity for the item.

Prices must be numeric values and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator. There is no fixed limit maximum price for an item.

By default, when you enter a price in any pricing column Price field, the eSuite software automatically places '1' as quantity in the Price quantity field. If the price is a split price, the quantity must reflect the item quantity for the Price field.

The Time field

The Time field specifies the exact time during which the Retail and Sale pricing columns are effective, which offers precision as to when the price becomes effective. For example, it can be used to specify a very short term promotional pricing, such as a 4-hour promotion.

The Time field is split into two (2) fields:

Retail pricing column

  1. The Retail start time that contains the start time for the retail price event.
  2. The Retail end time that contains the end time for the retail price event.

Sale pricing column

  1. The Sale start time that contains the start time for the sale price event.
  2. The Sale end time that contains the end time for the sale price event.

If no time is specified, the eSuite software uses 00:00 as the start time and 23:59 (11:59 pm) as the end time.
The TPR and Instore pricing columns do not include a time field. They automatically use 00:00 as the default start time, and 23:59 (11:59 pm) as the default end time.

Price method definitions

Here is the list of the price methods used by eSuite. Each price method is identified by one or two letters.

SIL extended price methods

Code Description
PF= Keep going package price

This price method is the same as the package method, except that once the package price quantity is reached, subsequent items continue to be sold at the package price.

SMS extended price methods

Code Description
RW= Reverse weight able

This price method inverts the scalability of the item.
This price method may be used when the item that is usually scalable is sold by unit during a promotion.

Note:
When a weight able item is sold by unit using the RW method, enter the average weight of the item in the Weight field of the Main item table. At the processing time, the quantity sold multiplied by the value of the Weight field will be used to affect correctly the quantity.
RQ= Reverse quantity required

This price method inverts the quantity required field.
This price method may be used when the item must request the quantity during a promotion only.
RD= Reverse discountable

This price method inverts the discount ability of the item.
This price method may be used to make a price level non discountable.
RP= Reverse quantity as unit

This price method inverts the price quantity usage.
The price quantity may be used to save in the total quantity and not for price calculation. For example, the item is 3/$1.00. When scanning one of these items, the amount will be $1.00 and the quantity will be 3.
RV= Reverse volume pricing

This price method inverts the functionality of the Package and Limit fields.

Note:
The Volume pricing mode may be set for all items in the System.ini file. See the reseller for more details.
RS= Reverse sale as discount

This price method sets the sale price as a discount so it can be part of the best discount feature. The calculated discount represents the difference between the active price and the regular price.
The promo as discount feature does not work with package and limited prices; when it is used, the package and limited prices are ignored. The promo as discount feature does not work with the best price level feature either. Other advanced pricing features might also be affected or not work with the promo as discount feature.
LT= Limit by transaction

This price method limits the number of this item or this electronic coupon given to a customer within a transaction.
The Limit field is used to specify the maximum amount or quantity accepted. See the Electronic coupons programming topic for more details on the electronic coupons.
LD= Limit by day

This price method limits the number of this item or this electronic coupon given to a customer within a day.
The Limit field is used to specify the maximum quantity accepted. See the Electronic coupon programming topic for more details on the electronic coupons.
LW= Limit by week

This price method limits the number of this item or this electronic coupon given to a customer within a week.
The Limit field is used to specify the maximum quantity accepted. See the Electronic coupon programming topic for more details on the electronic coupons.
LM= Limit by month

This price method limits the number of this item or this electronic coupon given to a customer within a month.
The Limit field is used to specify the maximum quantity accepted. See the Electronic coupon programming topic for more details on the electronic coupons.
LN= Limit by NRT

This price method limits the number of this item or this electronic coupon given to a customer based on his grand total sales.
The Limit field is used to specify the maximum quantity accepted. See the Electronic coupon programming topic for more details on the electronic coupons.
CU= Coupon unlimited

This price method removes the limits of the electronic coupon, i.e. the electronic coupon's discount is not matched to an item (or an entry), it is added as a new registration in the transaction, and more than one electronic coupon's discount may be given according to the same item.
This price method is used for electronic coupon items only.
CC= Coupon collision allowed

This price method allows electronic coupon's discount collisions, i.e. the electronic coupon's discount is still matched to an item (or an entry), but more than one electronic coupon's discount may be applied to this item (or entry).
This price method is used for electronic coupon items only.
CB= Coupon bundle price

This price method gives a bundle behavior to the electronic coupon, i.e. the amount of the electronic coupon becomes the price at which the group of items that composes the electronic coupon's criteria is sold.
This price method is used for electronic coupon items only. It can work in combination with the Accumulate for percent or bundle (A) electronic coupon method.
CR= Coupon reverse order

This price method inverts the matching of the electronic coupon from the cheapest item to the most expensive one, or from the most expensive item to the cheapest one.
By default, a fixed amount discount is matched with the most expensive item. With the CR price method, it is matched with the cheapest item.
By default, a percentage discount is matched with the cheapest item. With the CR price method, it is matched with the most expensive item.
By default, an item for free discount is matched with the cheapest item. With the CR price method, it is matched with the most expensive item.
CN= Coupon allow negative

This price method allows a negative discount (so increasing the value of the items) when the total value of the items of a bundle is lower than the value of the bundle.
For example, buy 5 meat items for $19.99. Since the meat items have variable weights, so variable values, the total value of the 5 meat items is always different. If the customer buys 5 meat items for which the total value is $17.99, the bundle e-coupon will increase the value of the 5 meat items to $19.99.
Px= Points program X

This price method identifies on which points programs the points defined in the Points field are awarded. More than one points program can be specified (e.g. P1P3).
In order to apply the points to the program X, the customer must be part of this points program (defined in the Shopper level table).

NOTE: The names of the points programs can be changed. More points programs can be listed (the system manages up to 35 points programs). See the reseller for more details.
M1=  Multiply points X

This price method allows to multiply the points awarded into the transaction using an electronic coupon, where x identifies the points program on which points are multiply. This method works in combination with the Points field that contains the multiplier factor.

Example:
M2 set in the Method field and 3 in the Points field means that points awarded on points program 2 are tripled.

By default, the points multiplication by an electronic coupon has priority over the points multiplication set into the system. This behavior can be changed. Refer to the reseller for more details.

The Retail (margin) field

The Retail (Margin) field contains the retail profit margin on the item in percentage. It is used to generate the item retail price.

There are two options to choose from in the Generate price utility - Cost table to calculate the retail (margin) price.

To calculate the margin from the retail price:

  • Retail TGM (Target Gross Margin) = cost / (1 - profit margin %)
  • Example: $3.33 = $10.00 / (1 - 25%)

To calculate the margin from the cost:

  • Retail TGM (Target Gross Margin) = cost + ( cost * margin)
  • Example: $2.50 = $10 + ($10 * 25%)

The Price (suggested retail) field

The Price (suggested retail) field contains the suggested retail price. It is used for information purposes only, and may appear in some reports. It is split in two fields:

  1. The Suggested retail price (SRP) amount field that contains the suggested retail price for the item, and
  2. The Suggested retail price (SRP) quantity field that contains the number of units included in the item. This field is grayed out; its value comes from the Sell pack field in the Main item table.

NOTE: When the price information is saved, eSuite automatically saves the sell pack quantity in the SRP quantity field. The default value of the sell pack quantity is 1.

The Exclude from spending field

The Exclude from spending (Exc. spend) field specifies whether the sales of the item are excluded from the points calculation (checked) or not (unchecked).

The Admissible spending totalizer is used to define the total sales amount admissible in the points calculation. When the Exclude from spending field is checked, the Admissible spending totalizer is not populated with the value of the item.

NOTES:
For this field to work, the item must not be programmed to follow the sub-department settings.
The Exclude from spending behavior may also be specified on keyed department sales and keyed sub-department sales.

The Like code field

The Like code field contains a code used to group items. It facilitates item maintenance as, when a change is made in the price record of an item that has a like code, the system can change the price record of all the other items having the same like code (for example, items that have different flavors, scents, etc.).

To enter a like code, type the UPC of the like code item in the field or click the arrow to open the Like code list and make a selection.
