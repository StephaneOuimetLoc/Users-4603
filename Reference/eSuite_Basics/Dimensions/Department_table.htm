eSuite - Department Table

eSuite > Dimensions > Departments > List

Information

The Department table contains information for groups of items. Some points of sale, especially cash registers, use department keys to sell non-coded items. The eSuite software uses departments as a way of grouping both coded items and direct key sales for reporting.

Instead of linking coded items directly to departments, eSuite uses sub-departments for grouping items, and then links the sub-departments to departments. This allows for another report level, regrouping item codes in a more specific manner.

The sub-departments also have their own settings, such as taxability, maximum and minimum entries for sales and voids etc., used when direct key sales are performed.

Displaying the Department table

In the Launcher, click the arrow in the Dimensions entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Departments: the Start window opens in the Department work area.
  2. Click on List: the Departmens tab opens in the same window, displaying the Department list.
  3. To modify an existing department, select it in the list and double-click on it, press the F3 key on the keyboard or click the Edit - Department F3 command in the bottom menu: the Department table opens, overlapping the work area.
  4. To create a new department, click on Add in the grid toolbar: the Department table opens, overlapping the work area.

Content

Here is a description of the Department table fields and their functionalities:

FIELD DESCRIPTION
Number The Number field contains the department number.
It is a required field used to uniquely identify records in the department list.
Description The Description field contains the description of the department.
It is not a required field; however, a description should be entered to identify the department in the department reports.
Group The Group field contains the department group, which organizes departments together for global sales reports.
It gives access to a drop-down list displaying the pre-established department groups users can choose among.
Alternate description The Alternate description field contains the Alternate description of the department.
It is not a required field.
Max. entry The Maximum entry field contains the maximum amount the cashiers can enter for the department key sales.
It prevents cashiers from entering too high a value in the department key.
Min. entry The Minimum entry field contains the minimum amount the cashiers can enter for the department key sales.
It prevents cashiers from entering too low a value in the department key.
Max. void The Maximum void field contains the maximum amount the cashiers can enter as a void (correction) for the department key sales.
It prevents overpunching void amounts at the points of sale.
Max. refund The Maximum refund field contains the maximum amount the cashiers can refund for the department key sales.
It prevents cashiers from refunding too high an amount in the department key.
Cust. age The Customer age field contains the minimum age the purchasers must be to buy any item in the department.
For details, see the Customer age field section below.
Cash. age The Cashier age field contains the minimum age the cashiers must be to perform sales in the department.
For details, see the Cashier age field section below.
Margin The Margin field contains the profit margin on the items in the department, in percentage.
Restrict. The Restrictor field links the department to a restriction that the direct department key sales must follow.
For example, in areas where stores are allowed to sell beer, wine or alcohol, sales might be legally restricted to certain hours of the day.
Commission The Commission field contains the salesperson's commission rate for the department.
Shrink The Shrink field contains the percentage that is allowed to be lost on a regular basis for the items in the department.
It is mostly used with perishable products.
Other flags The Other flags field contains additional department sales information.
For details, see the Other flags field section below.
Mem. no. The Memory number field contains the memory number that links the keyed department sales to the scales or the cash registers.
Priority The Priority field contains the order in which the department must appear in the specified modules.
Show filter The Show filter field contains the identification letters of the modules where the department must appear.
For details, see the Show filter field section below.
URL The URL field contains special parameters used when a sale is performed in the department.
Scalable The Scalable field specifies whether the department sales are performed by weight, scaled at the POS (checked) or not (unchecked).
Manual The Manual field specifies whether the weight of the scalable department sales can be manually entered using the POS keyboard (checked) or not (unchecked).
WIC item The WIC item field specifies whether the department sales are WIC able (checked) or not (unchecked).
Food stamp The Food stamp field specifies whether the department sales can be performed using food stamps as tender (checked) or not (unchecked).
FSA eligible The FSA eligible field specifies whether the department sales can be performed using an FSA card as tender (checked) or not (unchecked).
Not in net The Not in net field specifies whether the department sales are added to the store net sales (unchecked) or not (checked).
Exc. spending The Exclude from spending field specifies whether the keyed department sales are excluded from discounts and point programs (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 1 The Taxable 1 field specifies whether the department sales have the first tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 2 The Taxable 2 field specifies whether the department sales have the second tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 3 The Taxable 3 field specifies whether the department sales have the third tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 4 The Taxable 4 field specifies whether the department sales have the fourth tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 5 The Taxable 5 field specifies whether the department sales have the fifth tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 6 The Taxable 6 field specifies whether the department sales have the sixth tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Taxable 7 The Taxable 7 field specifies whether the department sales have the seventh tax for the retail region (checked) or not (unchecked).
Stop discount The Stop discount field specifies whether discounts may be applied on the department sales at the points of sale (unchecked) or not (checked).
For details, see the Stop discount field section below.
Stop return The Stop return field specifies whether the department sales should be returned (unchecked) or not (checked).
Stop refund The Stop refund field specifies whether the department sales should be refunded (unchecked) or not (checked).

The Customer age field

The Customer age (Cust. age) field contains the minimum age the customers must be of to buy any item in the department. For example, in areas where stores are allowed to sell beer, wine or alcohol, customers must be of legal adult age for that area in order to buy these items.

The customer age may also be specified for a whole sub-department or for an item only.

When a sale is performed in the department, the cashier is prompted to enter the customer's birth date at the POS. The system automatically calculates the customer's age and compares it to the value in the Customer age (Cust. age) field. If the customer is under the minimum age required, a warning message is displayed on the POS screen.

NOTES:
If the operator is a level 5 or higher, this restriction will be bypassed.
See the reseller regarding the possible alternate configurations (System.ini file).

The Cashier age field

The Cashier age (Cash. age) field contains the minimum age that the cashiers must be of to perform sales in the department. For example, in areas where stores are allowed to sell beer, wine or alcohol, cashiers must be of legal adult age for that area in order to sell these items.

The operator's age is verified based on the information entered in the Birth date field of the Operator table. If no birth date has been specified in this field, the system does not perform any age validation.

NOTE: The cashier's age can also be specified for a whole sub-department or for an item only.

The Other flags field

The Other flags field contains information on additional department sales. A department may have more than one value in the Other flags field.

NOTE: The Other flags information may also be specified for a whole sub-department or an item only.

To enter additional department sales information, users can type the abbreviation of the desired information in the Other flags field or double-click in the Other flags field to select the required information from the list of choices:

OTHER FLAGS INFORMATION ABBREVIATION DESCRIPTION
Enable zero price EZ The department sales accept zero prices in the price fields of the Price table ($0.00).
No cost plus price NC No cost plus price can be calculated for the department sales.
No update from import NI (information to come)
WIC 2 W2 The department sales are WIC able 2.
WIC 3 W3 The department sales are WIC able 3.
WIC 4 W4 The department sales are WIC able 4.
Web Order FE The item can be ordered from the Web.
Scale fix weight FW The scaled department sales have fixed weights (e.g. store-made muffins). See the scale manual for more information.
Scale fix weight by count FC The scaled department sales have fixed weight and is 'by count'.
Scale fix price SF The scaled department sales have fixed prices (e.g. store-made pizzas). See the scale manual for more information.
Scale special weight SW The scaled department sales are 'weighable' and 'by count'.
Scale open price SO The scaled department sales allow an operator to enter a price per pound/kilogram and the system will calculate the price based on the weight of the item.
Scale force tare FT The scaled department sales have a tare that is forced in the scale. See the scale manual for more information.


NOTE: More than one type of information may be entered, with no space in between the types of information.

The Show filter field

The Show filter field contains the identification letters of the modules where the department must appear.

The department will only appear in the specified modules, or in any of the modules if the Do Not Show value is entered.

To enter the modules where the department must appear, type their identification letters in the Show filter field or double-click in the Show filter field to select the modules from the list of choices:

Module Letter ID
Inventory I
Costing C
Forecast F
Budget B
Hour T

Supply inventory P
Packing purchase Y
Packing inventory U
Receiving R
Trend Book K
DoNotShow N
E-Commerce W


NOTE: More than one module may be entered, with no space in between.

The Stop discount field

The Stop discount field specifies whether discounts may be applied on the department sales at the points of sale (unchecked) or not (checked).

The Stop discount field does not allow manual item discounts, proportional discounts, global discounts, customer discounts (shopper level discounts) and sub-department discounts on the department sales.

NOTE: The Stop discount behavior may also be specified for a whole sub-department or an item only.

To prevent discounts on the department sales, check the Stop discount field.
