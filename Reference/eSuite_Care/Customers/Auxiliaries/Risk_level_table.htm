eSuite - Risk Level Table

eSuite > Customers > Master > General list

Information

the Risk level table contains the credit (store charge) or check cashing information. It is used to group customers by these common settings.

Each record of the Risk level table is programmed with a credit limit, used as a credit profile for each customer.

Risk levels

The Risk levels list displays all risk levels and related details. Risk levels are used to group the customers by credit information.

In the bottom menu:

  • Edit provides access to the Risk level table.

Below is a brief description of the information displayed by default in the Risk levels list.

COLUMN DESCRIPTION
Risk level The number corresponding to the risk level.
Customer risk print statement Displays the value 1 when the account statements are set to be printed.
Customer risk maximum credit The maximum credit amount allowed to the customers linked to the risk level.
Customer risk interest rate The amount of the interested expressed as a percentage, that must be added to the customer's account balance.
Customer risk description The description of the risk level.
Check limit The maximum check amount allowed by transaction to the customers linked to the risk level.
Check change limit The maximum change amount that can be given on a check for the customers linked to the risk level.
Check week limit amount The maximum check amount allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private card limit The maximum private card amount allowed by transaction to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private card weekly limit The maximum private card amount allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
Early payment discount The discount given to the customers when they pay their invoices within a certain number of days. 

Editing Risk levels

In the launcher, click the arrow in the Customers entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Master: the Start window opens in the Customer work area.
  2. Select General list.
  3. Enter the search criteria in the wizard and execute.
  4. Select a record in Customers - General list.
  5. Click Edit in the bottom menu and select Risk level: the Risk level table opens, overlapping the work area.

The Risk level table is also available from the Auxiliary tables entry under Customers. See the Customer auxiliaries topic for details.

Content

Here is the description of the Risk level table fields and their functionalities.

FIELDS DESCRIPTION
Main section
Risk level The Risk level field contains the risk level number.
It is a required field.
Descriptor The Descriptor field contains the description of the risk level.
Credit limit The Credit limit field contains the maximum credit amount allowed to the customers linked to the risk level.
The value entered must be numeric and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator.
Interest The Interest field contains the percentage of interest that must be added to the customer's account balance calculated on the amount due exceeding the Terms field value.
Statement The Statement field specifies whether the account statements are printed for the customers linked to the risk level (checked) or not (unchecked).
Invoice templatee The Invoice template field contains the name of the invoice template to use instead of the default one.
The invoice template file must exist in the \Storeman\Office\CGI directory. See the reseller for more details.
Copies The Copies field contains the number of statements to print for the customers that are part of the risk level.
Terms The Terms field contains the description of the payment terms.
Used for information purposes only. It may be displayed on statements. See the dealer for customization of statements.
Terms days The Terms days field contains the number of days within which the customer's invoices must be paid before interest will be charged on outstanding amounts.
It is used for information purposes only, except when the interest is calculated on a different period than monthly. the amount of interest is defined in the Interest field.
Days (Early payment) The Days (Early payment) field contains the number of days within which the customers have a discount if they pay their invoices. the discount given is defined in the Discount (Early payment) field.
Used for information purposes only. The system does not calculate any discount when a payment is made; it must be done manually.
Discount (Early payment) The Discount (Early payment) field contains the percentage of discount given to the customers when they pay their invoices within a number of days. the number of days is defined in the Days (Early payment) field.
Used for information purposes only. The system does not calculate any discount when a payment is made; it must be done manually.
Checks tab
Accept The Accept field contains the identification number of the check status for the customers linked to the risk level.
For details, see the Accept (Checks tab) field section below.
Limit The Limit field contains the maximum check amount allowed by transaction to the customers linked to the risk level.
The value entered must be numeric and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator.
Change The Change field contains the maximum change amount which can be given on a check for the customers linked to the risk level.
Week limit The Week limit field contains the maximum check amount allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
The value entered must be numeric and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator.
Quantity The Quantity field contains the maximum number of checks allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private card tab
Accept The Accept field contains the identification number of the private card status for the customers linked to the risk level.
For details, see the Accept (Private card) section below.
Limit The Limit field contains the maximum private card amount allowed by transaction to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private cards are cards managed within eSuite; their information is not sent to the bank for approval. They include in-house charge cards.
The value entered must be numeric and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator.
Week limit The Week limit field contains the maximum private card amount allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private cards are cards managed within eSuite; their information is not sent to the bank for approval. They include in-house charge cards.
The value entered must be numeric and must contain a decimal point as the dollars/cents separator.
Quantity The Quantity field contains the maximum number of times private cards allowed by week to the customers linked to the risk level.
Private cards are cards managed within eSuite; their information is not sent to the bank for approval. They include in-house charge cards.

The Accept field (Checks tab)

The Accept field contains the identification number of the check status for the customers linked to the risk level.

The following values are available in the drop-down list:

VALUE DESCRIPTION
Accept with limits Checks are accepted according to the Limit, Week limit and/or Quantitycriteria, with the positive/negative check file verification (if it is programmed). See your reseller for more details.
Call manager The intervention of the manager is needed to accept checks.
Never accept Checks are not accepted.
Always accept Checks are accepted without the positive/negative check file verification (even if it is programmed). See the dealer for more details.
