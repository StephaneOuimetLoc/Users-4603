eSuite - GS1 Databar Barcodes

eSuite > Items

Information

The GS1 Databar is a family of barcode symbologies. Some of them are small, intended for produce and small consumer packages. Some others are larger, intended to carry more data needed for identifying variable measure food and the required content on coupons.

Some of them can be omnidirectional (read from left to right and vice versa), making them perfectly suitable for the POS applications.

Four of the seven variants are omnidirectional. They are designed to work at the POS:

  • Databar Omnidirectional;
  • Databar Stacked Omnidirectional;
  • Databar Expanded; and
  • Databar Expanded Stacked

The three other variants are designed for very small products, such as health care items. They are not recommended to work at the POS.

The sections below provide details on the applications managed at the POS.

Packaged goods

The barcode used on the packaged goods is the GS1 Databar Omnidirectional barcode, or in some cases, the GS1 Databar Stacked Omnidirectional barcode.

It contains 14 digits that correspond to the Global Trade Item Number (GTIN) of the trade item. The GTIN has two main components: the company prefix of the manufacturer (assigned by GS1) and the item reference number (assigned by the manufacturer).

The UPC-A, UPC-E, EAN-13 and EAN-8 barcodes may continue to be used on packaged goods. The GS1 Databar barcode is smaller, easier to use on small packages and leaves more room for product information on large packages. This is the manufacturers' choice.

The value of the barcode may or may not be human readable.

The GS1 Databar barcode contains:

GTIN 13 GTIN 12

If the barcode is not human readable, scan the barcode with a PDA or a scanner. Enter only 13 digits in the system maintenance. Start with the first digit on the left (even if it is zero) and enter all following digits except the check digit (the last one).

Produce

The barcode used on fresh produce is the GS1 Databar Stacked Omnidirectional barcode. It contains 14 digits that correspond to the Global Trade Item Number (GTIN) of the fresh produce. The GTIN has two main components: the company prefix of the manufacturer (assigned by GS1) and the item reference (assigned by the manufacturer).

The 4-digit UPC used up to now is printed next to the barcode for a compatibility with today's process. The values encoded in the barcode are not human readable.

The GS1 Databar produce barcode contains:

USA and Canada Other countries
1. Leading zeros (2 digits);
2. The company prefix (6 to 9 digits);
3. The item reference (2 to 5 digits);
4. The check digit (1 digit).
 1. Leading zero (1 digit);
2. The company prefix (7 to 10 digits);
3. The item reference (2 to 5 digits);
4. The check digit (1 digit).

In the system maintenance, enter the 4-digit UPC as the main PLU used for the item. This way, the cashiers can continue to enter manually the UPC at the POS. The system will automatically expand the UPC to 13 digits by adding nine zeros (000000000) at the beginning when the Execute button is clicked in the wizard.

The GS1 Databar produce barcode is not human readable and it must be scanned with a PDA or a scanner. Enter the encoded value in the Alternate code table without adding the zeros at the beginning of the code or the check digits at the end. Because produce can come from many companies, the same produce may have more than one alternate code. At the POS, the cashier has two seconds to place the produce on the scale after they scan the barcode.

NOTES:
The item reference in the barcode may be the 4-digit PLU or any other value defined by the manufacturer.
The 2-digit Application Identifier (01) is not encoded into the GS1 Databar Stacked Omnidirectional barcode.

Variable measure food

The barcode used on the variable measure food is the GS1 Databar Expanded or GS1 Databar Expanded Stacked barcode. Since it can contain up to 74 digits, the weight, the extended price, the best before date and the lot number can be encoded in the barcode.

Coupons

The barcode used on coupons is the GS1 Databar Expanded or GS1 Databar Expanded Stacked barcode. Since it can contain up to 74 digits, the conditions of the coupon and the save value, the offer code and the expiration date can be encoded in the barcode.

The values encoded in the barcode are not human readable. Only the company prefix and the offer code are displayed above the barcode. The value of the coupon, its conditions and the expiration date are printed on the coupon.

The GS1 Databar coupon barcode can contain up to 74 digits:

  1. The application identifier (4 digits)
  2. The primary company prefix (6 to 12 digits)
  3. The offer code (6 digits)
  4. The save value (1 to 5 digits)
  5. The primary purchase requirement (1 to 5 digits)
  6. The primary purchase family code (3 digits)
  7. The second purchase requirement - optional (1 to 5 digits)
  8. The second purchase family code - optional (3 digits)
  9. The second company prefix - optional (6 to 12 digits)
  10. The third purchase requirement - optional (1 to 5 digits)
  11. The third purchase family code - optional (3 digits)
  12. The third company prefix - optional (6 to 12 digits)
  13. The expiration date - optional (6 digits)
  14. The start date - optional (6 digits)
  15. The serial number - optional (6 to 15 digits)
  16. The retailer identification - optional (7 to 13 digits)
  17. What represents the save value (1 digit)
  18. On which item the save value is applied (1 digit)
  19. The coupon type - vendor or store (1 digit)
  20. If the offer can be multiplied (1 digit)

The eSuite software can retrieve the encoded information when the coupon is scanned at the POS and validate its conditions. See the reseller for details.

NOTE: Some information of the GS1 Databar coupon is optional. This means the cashier still has to validate the coupon before scanning it, as it is not possible to know what is encoded in the barcode and what is not. Sometimes, the encoding requires the intervention of the cashier. In that case, the "Process manually" message is displayed in the Entry window of the POS and the cashier has to use the vendor coupon function manually to give the discount. They can also refuse it. See the reseller for details.
