Copy and paste data

Information

The eSuite Copy and Paste functionality allows grabbing Customer, Items & Batch relevant data from e-mail, Word, Note Pad documents, CSV or Excel spread sheets. Paste it in the following Wizards in eSuite to view Customers, Items, Batches & Imports data. It also allows the creation of Batches for Price batch, Cost batch and or External Batches.

This example below is External Batches, you will easily find this in the Batch module under the section of Imports>External Batches.

Once you have clicked on "Create Batch with Copy/Paste" button a "Batch Create" Tab will be propmt. You may enter the Description, Type, Action, State and Start date & time as seen below in the red rectangle. As for the data you wish to "Copy paste" this can be done in the yellow highlighter section. You simply need to click you mouse inside the highlight area and your the "Ctrl + V" action in order to have your data displayed.

NOTE: Be sure that when you copy over your data text (ex: Excel, NotePad) that you have a Header row as this is required in order to map the associated fields to capture the details you desire.

After you have entered your detail in the mentioned fields above and pasted your data in the correct area you will then be able to start the associating the hearder fields.

Mouse over the Header column and this will prompt a pull down menu where you will have the options Search the fields you required and then you will need to associate the selected fields to each column.

Click on "Search Fields" this will prompt at "Map Fields" window where you can choose to select from a Table (ex OBJ_TAB) or should you know the field name you want you can enter that in the "Field" box (ex: F01 for UPC) or again by the field "Description"





If checked and selected teh "Add to List" button it will apprear in the Asscoiated fields as a option to be selected to the columns you are on.

After Mapping your columns you will notice all have the fields ID's in Brackets

At this point you would then click on the "Checkmark" to Save (located in the Header menu top right corner) and completed your created External Batch. Prompting a new Batch Items Tab

At this point you with then be able to find your created External Batch from the "Links" Start window and click on External Batches in order to view and execute when you desire.

Opening the List Batch Wizard which is deflauted to view "Open" batch Statuses. You may quick click on Execute
