eSuite - Electronic Coupon Link Table

eSuite > Items > Master > Item list

Information

The Coupon link table is used to program and modify the criteria of an existent electronic coupon, i.e. the conditions that must be met in order to get the electronic coupon discount.

Electronic coupons have their own UPCs, just like the items do, but they have different settings that identify them as electronic coupons. An electronic coupon may have an unlimited number of criteria. Each criterion must be fulfilled in order to get the electronic coupon discount (an AND link between all of them, no possibility to get an OR link instead).

For details on how to create electronic coupons, see the How to create an electronic coupon topic.

NOTE: eSuite must be reloaded after the creation of that first coupon link to check whether there is any coupon link in the system; if there is none, it disables the coupon link update engine to speed up the transaction process.

Advice

The Quantity, Amount and Weight fields are used as minimum conditions in the criteria. Only one of them could be used in a criterion. If two minimum conditions are needed on the same PID, use a second PID for the second condition.

For example, for an electronic coupon that gives 3 random weight items for $10.00, you should want to validate the total amount of these 3 items to be sure it is at least $10.00. In that case, the random weight items are part of 2 PIDs and the electronic coupon has 2 criteria: one for the quantity of 3 based on the first PID and a second one for the amount of $10.00 based on the second PID.

Displaying the Electronic coupon link table

In the Launcher, click the arrow in the Items entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Master: the Start window opens in the Items work area.
  2. Click on Item list: the Wizard tab opens in the same window, displaying the Items - General List.
  3. Enter the search criteria and click Execute: results will display in a record grid, in the Items tab of the same window.
  4. Select a record and click on Edit on the bottom of the item grid: the option menu is displayed.
  5. Select Special items and then Electronic coupon link: the Coupon link edit table opens overlapping the work area.

Layout

The Coupon link table is divided in two parts: the main section and the Coupon link table grid.

Main section

* Click on a field to get information on it.

Coupon link table grid

* Click on a field to get information on it.

The Coupon link table grid offers quick information on the types and the code of the electronic coupon criterion. The following columns are displayed: Type and Code.

Columns are movable, resizable and may be selected/deselected for a customized experience. They allow sorting the records in an ascending or a descending order.
Records are displayed in alphabetical order by type. Selecting a record allows displaying and editing its information in the main section of the Coupon link table.

The Type field

The Type field contains the abbreviation of the type of the criterion which needs to be respected in order to get the electronic coupon.

Type Description
ITM

The Item sales criterion is based on the sales of a certain item.
PID The Promo ID sales criterion is based on the sales of a group of items.

The items are grouped using a promo ID number that is set in the POS table (Promotion ID field) for each item.
SUB

The Sub-department sales criterion is based on the sales of a certain sub-department.
DPT

The Department sales criterion is based on the sales of a certain department.
TTL The Total sales criterion is based on the sales amount accumulated in the totalizer in the Totalizer (Tlz) field.
PPTS

The Minimum point balance criterion is based on the balance of minimum points accumulated by the customer.
CLT

The Customer specific criterion is based on a specific customer.
CLF

The Customer level criterion is based on a certain customer level.
CNF

The Customer not level criterion excludes a certain customer level.
CLG

The Customer group criterion is based on a certain customer group.
CG2

The Customer group 2 criterion is based on a certain customer group 2.
CNT

The Every X transaction criterion is based on a number of transactions performed on a same POS.
RND

The Random with X chances criterion is based on a random possibility.

The Method field

The Method field contains the identification letters of the electronic coupon methods used, corresponding to a special electronic coupon behavior.

The available methods are:

Identifier Behavior
 

Empty
Basically, an electronic coupon is only given once in a transaction, even if its criteria is matched many times. Each item sold may participate in more than one electronic coupon, but only one (1) electronic coupon's discount may be applied on an item (or an entry.

Examples:
Electronic coupon #1: 2 Sprite                           = $0.30 off
Electronic coupon #2: 1 Coke + 1 Chips             = $0.50 off
Electronic coupon #3: 1 Coke + 1 Chocolate bar = $0.40 off

  • The customer #1 buys 2 Sprite. He will get $0.30 off.
  • The customer #2 buys 4 Sprite. He will get $0.30 off.
  • The customer #3 buys 1 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
  • The customer #4 buys 2 Coke, 2 Chips and 2 Chocolate bars. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
  • The customer #5 buys 2 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
R

Multiply using criteria
The Multiply using criteria (R) method specifies that the electronic coupon is given each time the criterion is reached.

The Multiply using criteria method has to be specified for each criterion that it applies to.

Examples:
Electronic coupon #1: 2 Sprite                           = $0.30 off
Electronic coupon #2: 1 Coke + 1 Chips             = $0.50 off
Electronic coupon #3: 1 Coke + 1 Chocolate bar = $0.40 off

with the R method for each criterion.

The customer #1 buys 2 Sprite. He will get $0.30 off.

The customer #2 buys 4 Sprite. He will receive $0.60 (2 x $0.30) off.

The customer #3 buys 1 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.

The customer #4 buys 2 Coke, 2 Chips and 2 Chocolate bar. He will get $1.80 (2 x $0.50 + 2 x $0.40) off.

The customer #5 buys 2 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
Q

Multiply using sales
The Multiply using sales (Q) method specifies that the electronic coupon is given for each matched item sold when the electronic coupon's criterion is matched.

The Multiply using sales method is useful when you want to give a discount per pound when a minimum poundage is reached, since the Multiply using sales method gives fractional quantities.

The Multiply using sales method has to be specified for each criterion that it applies to.

Examples:
Electronic coupon #1: 2 Sprite                           = $0.30 off
Electronic coupon #2: 1 Coke + 1 Chips             = $0.50 off
Electronic coupon #3: 1 Coke + 1 Chocolate bar = $0.40 off

with the Q method for each criterion.

  • The customer #1 buys 2 Sprite. He will get $0.60 (2 x $0.30) off.
  • The customer #2 buys 4 Sprite. He will receive $1.20 (4 x $0.30) off.
  • The customer #3 buys 1 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
  • The customer #4 buys 2 Coke, 2 Chips and 2 Chocolate bar. He will get $1.80 (2 x $0.50 + 2 x $0.40) off.
  • The customer #5 buys 2 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $1.80 (2 x $0.50 + 2 x $0.40) off.

Fractional quantities example:
Electronic coupon #4: 2 pounds of meat = $0.10 per pound off.

The customer #6 buys 2.65 pounds of meat. He will get $0.27 (2.65 * $0.10) off.

Note:
For the Code 2 items (pre-scaled or random weight items), the Scalable field of the POS table must be checked ON on the item definitions. Otherwise, no discount will be given on these items since the system is not able to know how many pounds (or kilograms) are sold.

Warning:
Avoid using the Q (Multiply using sales) method with a minimum amount of $0.01 as condition. In that case, the system can issue a fractional quantity of the e-coupon, so not giving the expected result. A minimum quantity should be used instead.
S

Multiply using smallest
The Multiply using smallest (S) method specifies that when an item of that criterion is sold more than once in a transaction, the electronic coupon is given according to the smallest item, based on the weight.

This method is useful when you want to give a discount per pound on the smallest item.
B

Multiply using biggest
The Multiply using biggest (B) method specifies that when an item of that criterion is sold more than once in a transaction, the electronic coupon is given according to the biggest item, based on the weight.

This method is useful when you want to give a discount per pound on the biggest item.
C

Multiply using cheapest
The Multiply using cheapest (C) method specifies that when an item of that criterion is sold more than once in a transaction, the electronic coupon is given according to the cheapest item, based on the amount.
L

Limited participation
The Limited participation (L) method specifies that the items of that criterion may be associated to only one (1) electronic coupon in a transaction. In this case, the system looks for the best electronic coupon, based on its classification.

This method applies with the Item sales (ITM), Promo id sales (PID), Sub-department (SUB), Department sales (DPT) and Total sales (TTL) types.

The Limited participation method has to be specified for each criterion that it applies to.

Examples:
Electronic coupon #1: 2 Sprite                           = $0.30 off
Electronic coupon #2: 1 Coke + 1 Chips             = $0.50 off
Electronic coupon #3: 1 Coke + 1 Chocolate bar = $0.40 off

with the L method for each criterion.

  • The customer #1 buys 2 Sprite. He will receive $0.30 off.
  • The customer #2 buys 4 Sprite. He will receive $0.30 off.
  • The customer #3 buys 1 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.50 off.
  • The customer #4 buys 2 Coke, 2 Chips and 2 Chocolate bars. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
  • The customer #5 buys 2 Coke, 1 Chips and 1 Chocolate bar. He will get $0.90 ($0.50 + $0.40) off.
X

Exclude this from total
The Exclude this from total (X) method specifies that the items that are part of the criterion are excluded from the other criteria.

It is used on the Item sales (ITM), Sub-department sales (SUB) and Department sales (DPT) types to exclude these items from other criteria defined with the Promo Id sales (PID), the Sub-department sales (SUB), the Department sales (DPT) and the Total sales (TTL) types (exclude the smallest groupings from the biggest groupings).
Items of a Promo Id sales (PID) can be excluded from the Total sales (TTL) type only.

NOTE: The exclusion is based on the same totalizer (totalizer 3 on both criteria, or totalizer 7 on both criteria, etc.).

WARNING: If the discount is set in the TPR, Sale or Instore price column and the discount is over (i.e. the current date is after the ending date), the system does not consider the conditions of the e-coupon if the Retail price is $0.00 and the EZ value IS NOT set in the Other flags field of the POS table. This means the exclusions set on the invalid e-coupons will not interfere with the valid ones.
M

Match with this item
The Match with this item (M) method forces to which item (or entry) the electronic coupon's discount is applied.

If no match is forced, the electronic coupon's discount is applied by the system to any available item in the transaction according to the smallest impact on the item sales.

If the electronic coupon's discount cannot be applied on an item (or an entry), the electronic coupon's discount is not given.

It is also possible to match the discount to more than one criterion (up to a maximum of 3). In this case, the discount given is split proportionally between the specified items. This works for dollar discounts and bundle discount; this does not work for percentage discount and items for free. See the Electronic coupons - Examples topic for an example.

NOTE: Basically, only one (1) electronic coupon's discount may be applied to an item (or an entry). Be sure that you do not create collisions.
A

Accumulate for percent or bundle
The Accumulate for percent or bundle (A) method specifies from which item (or entry) the electronic coupon's discount expressed as a percentage is calculated.

The Accumulate for percent or bundle method can also be used on bundle e-coupons, i.e. selling a group of items at a special price. In this case, the price of the electronic coupon is not considered as a discount, but as the price at which the bundle is sold. Note that for the electronic coupon to have a bundle behavior, the Coupon bundle price (CB) method must be specified in the Method field of the Price table.

In the case where the items that are part of the bundle have different prices, the system will use the average price of the items sold to calculate the discount given by the bundle e-coupon. To use the exact price of all items sold instead, the A method should not be used (only the CB method).

NOTES:
The Match with this item (M) method can be useful with the Accumulate for percent or bundle (A) method.
The Accumulate for percent or bundle (A) method mixed with the Multiply using sales (Q) method means that the percentage is given according to the sales. This mix is used with the Promo Id sales (PID), the Sub-department (SUB), the Department sales (DPT) and the Total sales (TTL) types to give a percentage on an accumulated amount.
N Not count
The Not count (N) method is used to tell the system to do not count the criterion to determine if the e-coupon should be given, but subtract it if the e-coupon is given.

Example:
Electronic coupon #1: Get a Pepsi 500 ml for free;
Electronic coupon #2: Get 2 500 ml for $2.50.

PID 61 includes Pepsi 500 ml.
PID 62 includes Pepsi 500 ml, Coke 500 ml and 7Up 500 ml.

Because the Pepsi 500 ml item is part of the 2 promotions, it is part of the 2 PIDs. However, if the Pepsi 500 ml is given for free, it should not count for the bundle discount. This is where the N method is used.

Get a Pepsi 500 ml for free   Get 2 500 ml for $2.50
Type Code Cum Tlz Qty Meth   Type Code Cum Tlz Qty Meth
PID 61 T 3 1     PID 62 T 3 2  
              PID 61 T 3 1 N


NOTE: The methods may be mixed, except for some of them: RQ, SB, SC and BC.

The Cumulative field

The Cumulative (Cum.) field contains the cumulative identifier, used to specify that the minimum quantity, amount or weight required to get the electronic coupon comes from a transaction or a cumulative of many transactions. Its default value is T (for transaction).

The cumulative identifiers are:

Identifier Description
T

Transaction
The Transaction (T) identifier specifies that the quantity, amount or weight required to get the coupon comes from the transaction.
D

Daily
The Daily (D) identifier specifies that the quantity, amount or weight required to get the coupon comes from the cumulative of many transactions performed within a day for a customer.

The system needs to query how many items the customer has purchased during the day, so a customer number must be entered for each transaction.
W

Weekly
The Weekly (W) identifier specifies that the quantity, amount or weight required to get the coupon comes from the cumulative of many transactions performed within a week for a customer.

The system needs to query how many items the customer has purchased during the week, so a customer number must be entered for each transaction.
M

Monthly
The Monthly (M) identifier specifies that the quantity, amount or weight required to get the coupon comes from the cumulative of many transactions performed within a month for a customer.

The system needs to query how many items the customer has purchased during the month, so a customer number must be entered for each transaction.
N

Grand total
The Grand total (N) identifier specifies that the quantity, amount or weight required to get the coupon comes from the cumulative of all transactions for a customer.

The system needs to query how many items the customer has purchased since the beginning, so a customer number must be entered for each transaction.

NOTES:
Be careful when setting the cumulative field to Daily, Weekly, Monthly and Grand total: since the POS needs to send a request to the backoffice database to get the movement for the specified items, this can slow down the tendering process.
The system automatically cumulates the number of items purchased by each customer, when the cumulative identifier is different than Transaction (T). This means that the Capture data field in the POS table does not need to be checked for these items.
