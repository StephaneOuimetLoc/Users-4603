eSuite - Kit Table

eSuite > Items > Master > Item list

Information

Document needs revising and updating once the Generate ratio feature is available in eSuite (Raluca, 2019-08-19)

The Kit table is used to join multiple items and sell them as one item. It is used to manage kits that contain different items. Kits are considered items since they have their own UPCs; the major difference is that they include other items. A kit may contain an unlimited number of items. Each of these items must already exist in the system.

The present document describes the Kit table fields and functionalities. It explains how to create kits in eSuite and how to generate ratios automatically.

Displaying the Kit table

In the Launcher, click the arrow in the Items entry to display its menu and then:

  1. Click on Master: the Start window opens in the Items work area.
  2. Click on Item list: the Wizard tab opens in the same window, displaying the Items - General List.
  3. Enter the search criteria and click Execute: results will display in a record grid, in the Items tab of the same window.
  4. Select a record and click on Edit on the bottom of the item grid: the option menu is displayed.
  5. Select Special items and then Kit: the Kit edit table opens overlapping the work area.

Layout

The Kit table is divided in two parts: the main section and the Kit table grid.

Main section

* Click on a field to get information on it.

Kit table grid

* Click on a field to get information on it.

The Kit table grid offers quick information on the items that are part of the kit. The following columns are displayed: Level and Linked UPC.

Columns are movable, resizable and may be selected/deselected for a customized experience. They allow sorting the records in an ascending or a descending order.
By default, records are displayed in ascending order by level. Selecting a record allows displaying and editing its information in the main section of the Kit table.

Content

Here is a description of the item Kit table fields and their functionalities:

Creating a kit

A kit is an item containing other items. For example, a gift basket that contains a chocolate bar, bottle of wine and a book.
Inventory is not kept on the gift basket but on the individual items in the gift basket. When selling a gift basket, the individual items are reduced.

After creating the first kit in the system, eSuite must be reloaded. The software checks if there are any kits in the system and if not, it disables the kit update engine to speed up the transaction process.

The Kit table is used to manage the kit. For kits that contain many of the same item, like a carton of cigarettes, use the Split code and Split quantity fields of the Cost table.

To create a kit, follow these steps:

  1. Create a new item (the kit).
  2. Select the kit record in the item list. Open the Kit table.
  3. Click Add in the toolbar to add a new item to the kit.
    The Item field is populated with the kit UPC.
    The Level field displays 1 (Regular).
  4. If necessary, change the price level.
  5. In the Linked UPC field, enter the item code, or

NOTE: The ratio value can also be entered manually. The Ratio sale field is left blank for the last item of the kit for rounding purposes. The system will calculate it to 100 minus the ratios of the other items in the kit. The Ratio sale field of the other items must not be blank. If the kit retail price is 10.00, the system will not know what it is supposed to be calculating and will enter the total retail price of the kit for each of its items.

Generating ratios

Each item that composes a kit has a ratio sale. This ratio indicates the percentage the item holds in the kit, calculated from the ratio of the individual item price on the kit price. It defines the price for each item of the kit, for the item sales reports to correctly reflect the sales amounts. The values of the ratios may be generated automatically.

The Ratio sale field is left blank for the last item of the kit for rounding purposes.

To generate automatically the ratio for all items in the kit, click on the Generate ratio button.

How generating automatic ratio works

The system calculates the ratio sale for the first item of the kit, i.e. the ratio of the item price on the kit price. It continues with the second item, and so on until the next to last item. No ratio is calculated for the last item of the kit for rounding purposes. The last item of the kit absorbs the rebate given by the kit.

NOTES:
  • When the kit only includes one item, no ratio is calculated.
  • The order in which the items are added in the Kit table becomes significant with generating the automatic ratio.

Examples

Let us see some examples of the automatic ratio generation.

Example 1

A gift basket that includes three bottles of wine is sold for $20.00. Individually, each bottle is sold $10.00. Images below shown as example; please change them with the equivalents in eSuite when Generate ratio available.

After generating the ratios automatically, the ratio of bottle 1 is 50 ($10.00/$20.00 = 50%), and the ratio of bottle 2 is 50 ($10.00/$20.00 = 50%). The Ratio sale field of bottle 3 is left blank for rounding purposes. The last item of the kit absorbs the rebate given by the kit and bottle 3 is considered free.

Example 2

A gift basket that includes 1 bottle of shampoo, 1 bottle of conditioner and 2 soaps is sold for $5.50. Individually, the bottle of shampoo is sold $2.50, the bottle of conditioner is sold $2.50 and the soaps are sold $1.00 each.

After generating the ratios automatically, the ratio of the bottle of shampoo is 45.45 ($2.50/$5.50 = 45.45%), and the ratio of the bottle of conditioner is 45.45 ($2.50/$5.50 = 45.45%). The Ratio sale field of the 2 soaps is left blank for rounding purposes. The last item of the kit absorbs the rebate given by the kit, so in this case, the 2 soaps are sold $0.25 each ($5.50 - $2.50 - $2.50 = $0.50/2 = $0.25).
