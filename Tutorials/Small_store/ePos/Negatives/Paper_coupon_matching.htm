Tenders: vendor and store coupons

The Vendor coupon and Store coupon tenders do not try to match the paper coupons with items. They do not verify if enough items have been purchased to accept the paper coupons, but the system may force the cashiers to press a department or sub-department key or to scan an item (WIT=REG alpha parameter) to borrow the taxability, the food stamp ability or the WIC ability of the linked items (TAX alpha parameter).

The vendor and store coupons can be manually entered at the ePOS using the Vendor coupon and Store coupon buttons.

Store coupon

Bellow are the steps to enter a Store coupon by calling the button at the POS:

  1. Open an epos session and login
  2. Sell and Item
  3. Press the Store coupon
  4. The coupon should attach itself to the item.

Function: Store and vendor coupon

Store and vendor coupons used as a tender in the transaction is considered as a payment. The system will not go through any validation to accept the coupon.

However, it is possible to force the cashier to press a sub-department or scan an item for the system to borrow the taxability.

The function used are 108 for the vendor coupon and 107 for the store coupon.

Functions (vendor and store coupons)

The Vendor coupon and Store coupon functions try to match the paper coupons with items, i.e. they verify that enough items have been purchased to accept the paper coupons. By default, the coupon functions affect an existing line in the transaction.

The vendor and store coupons can be manually entered at the POS using the Vendor coupon and Store coupon function keys or the system can be programmed to automatically use the coupon functions when vendor and store coupons are scanned (System.ini/Function link). See the reseller for more details.

The behavior of the system is different when the paper coupons are entered manually and when they are scanned.

Paper coupons entered manually

By default, the system subtracts the paper coupon value from the current registration. The desired item needs to be selected in the Detail window prior to entering the paper coupon value.

The system may force the cashiers to press a department or sub-department key or to scan the affected item (WIT=REG alpha parameter) after the paper coupon is entered. This way, the system automatically locates and affects the first matching item of the transaction.

Paper coupons scanned

When a paper coupon is scanned, the system uses the UCC standard matching to find which line or which item of the transaction is affected. In this standard, the system tries to find any item that has the same company number (manufacture code) as the paper coupon code. Once it finds the matching company number, it tries to match the family code. The family code is programmable for each item in the Family field of the POS table.

If the item is not programmed with a family code, the system tries to match the company number.

If the paper coupon gives an item for free, a family code must be programmed for the item because without the family code, the system cannot be sure to match the paper coupon with the right item. In this case, the system does not accept the paper coupon.

If a paper coupon cannot be matched, the Over registration error message is displayed in the Entry window.

NOTES:

  • By default, vendor coupons are displayed as new registrations in the transaction so they do not affect the price of the items. The store coupons affect the items. In both cases, the validation of the paper coupons applies.
  • The system requires a family code only for paper coupons that give items for free or a percentage discount. However, the system can be configured to always validate the family code.
  • The scanned paper coupons are checked by quantity to ensure that the paper coupons do not outnumber the purchased items.
  • On the Get one free paper coupon type, the cheapest item that matches the paper coupon is given away.
  • The Buy x or more paper coupon type is implemented so the paper coupon cannot be scanned more than once in the same transaction.
